WHY COMMUNISM?·ESSAYSa voice-led essay

a voice-led essay · 22 july 2026 · why communism?

EverythingandNothing

or, what runs from a worker’s hand to the edge of the universe?

by eleanor aster

A while back, I pressed record and followed a thought out loud. It began with someone saying “wait,” then kept growing faster than I could write. I have kept that spoken journey here, only shaping it enough for you to follow beside me.

Without really thinking about it, you are separating us.

You are taking longer in the bathroom while I am asking you to come into the living room. What are you doing in there? Maybe showering. Maybe brushing your teeth. Maybe just taking your time. I want you over here because I want us to talk more clearly.

You say, “Wait.”

That little word creates a push and pull. I am drawing us together. You are keeping us apart for a little longer. I am trying to make the moment arrive faster. You are slowing it down. To lengthen and to shorten.

I hurry you up. You delay.

Then you finish, and now we are sitting in the living room together. So I got what I wanted. But the contradiction did not disappear just because you entered the room. I raised my voice. You arrived tired and anxious. I wanted closeness, but the way I forced us together put something between us.

We are happy with each other again. Still, just a moment ago, I rushed you and you slowed down against me.

I am not a genius. I am only looking closely at this tiny thing we did. Somehow, it is in our DNA.

That sounds ridiculous when I say it this early. Fine. Let me try.

Let us stop for a moment and think of a cell.

Before the cell, the ocean is full of pieces that are not alive yet. Water. Minerals. Carbon compounds. Protein-like molecules forming, breaking, meeting something else. There is activity everywhere, but most of it disappears into the sea.

Then one thing made by a reaction stays close enough to help the next reaction happen. The second leaves something the third can use. Work begins to build on work. The little chemical world does not have to start over every single time.

But how does it keep any of this? The ocean can scatter everything it makes.

Maybe it happens deep below, around the hot vents in the seafloor. Tiny spaces in the rock hold the pieces near each other. The heat keeps them reacting. Something can finally build because the products do not wash away immediately.

The pieces have begun to organize. Not because they sat down and chose a leader. They are simply held together long enough for the result of one process to become the material of another. Their labour accumulates.

But it is still trapped in the rock.

So what does it do?

How do you protect what has gathered and carry it into the ocean without losing it?

Maybe a shield, right?

Certain molecules can gather into a thin boundary of their own. The reactions now have an inside that can move. The boundary keeps some things in and lets other things cross. A cell is a little circle with a shield. We call it a membrane.

Think about what the cell does. It takes material from outside, works on it, builds itself, and releases a changed material back into the ocean. The world enters the cell. The labour of the cell enters the world.

The ocean shapes life, yes, but life is already shaping the ocean back.

Then the ocean reacts even harder.

The shield breaks. The heat changes. Food disappears. Another life tries to consume it. The dead does not need a mind in order to press against the living. A wave does not hate the cell. It still destroys it.

So what does life do?

It hides where the conditions let it remain. It changes. It uses whatever the world gives it. The water destroys us, so we take it in.

And a shield is not enough. They need to group together.

One ancient cell takes another inside. The relation could have begun through capture, even an attempt to eat it, but the relation holds. One brings powers the other does not have. Over an enormous time, the descendants of the smaller cell become the mitochondria inside the larger cell.

The labour of one life is now working inside another.

This is what I mean by organization. When they suffer alone and cannot win alone, their different powers become a larger power together.

Not by theory. By the contradiction they are living through.

And then the Sun.

The Sun is dangerous. It can damage life. But life does not only hide from it forever. Some cells develop pigments and machinery that catch its light. Photosynthesis takes photons from the dead world and turns their energy into the means of producing life.

The sunlight becomes embodied in the cell.

That is wild. The thing striking life from outside becomes a tool inside it. Life turns what is not alive into the material and energy of more life.

One cell doing that is tiny. Then it divides. It happens again. And again. Living activity changes the chemistry of the ocean, then the atmosphere. Oxygen accumulates because life keeps producing it.

Now the world is different.

And that changed world presses back. Oxygen is deadly to much of the life formed before it. Other life can use it to release more energy. What life produced becomes a danger to life, then a new power of life.

Do you see? Life works on the world. The worked world returns and works on life.

I promise, I will get to humanity very soon.

First, how does any of this continue after the individual cell dies?

DNA.

It is a secret message from life to life. A material memory. Not a perfect memory, because it changes when it is copied, but enough of the past enters the next living thing that life no longer begins from nothing.

DNA is theory before words ever existed. It carries a record of relations that continued to work. Reproduction copies it. Sex brings different inheritances together. The message travels forward, changes, and gets tested again by the world.

Life remembers before it has a brain.

Then the cells keep organizing. Not only one cell living inside another, but many cells whose different labour becomes one organism.

One part takes in food. Another moves. Another holds the boundary together. Each becomes more dependent on the others, and together they produce a life that none of them could be alone.

Organizing cells. Organism.

The word was sitting there the whole time.

Now the organism can move through the water. It can sense changes before they destroy it. It can chase food and escape what chases it. Every power it develops changes what it can do to the world around it.

And this is important because evolution is often imagined as the environment doing everything while the organism waits to be shaped.

But life is active.

Put yourself in the eyes of the individual. You swim. You eat. You hide. You change where the nutrients go. You leave waste. You kill something or become food for something else. Your activity changes your body while you are alive, and it changes the conditions in which the next generation has to live.

The environment shapes the organism through a world that living activity has already helped shape.

Different individuals do not live that world in exactly the same way. Their bodies differ. Their experiences differ. Some variations let their descendants continue under the changed conditions. Across generations, the life of individuals becomes a changed species.

Nature, labour, and the individual are all inside the same movement.

Hold on, because I do not want this to become a ladder with humanity waiting at the top.

The ocean was not secretly trying to produce us. Most lineages do not march toward greater complexity. They branch. They specialize. Some become simpler. Most disappear.

I am following one path among many, the one that happened to reach a life capable of looking back. It could have broken at any point. It can still fail.

We can look back and see what the earlier world made possible. But possible is not promised. Nothing back there guaranteed that we would arrive.

Let us keep going.

The ocean fills with life. Living things consume the materials around them and one another. The water that made them possible also holds them in. The edge of the land becomes both danger and possibility.

Life reaches it.

Not in one heroic jump. Living things work at the border for generation after generation. Some remain in shallow water. Some survive in mud. Small inherited differences become larger differences because the world keeps testing them through actual life.

Eventually the body itself records the struggle. Fins that moved through water give way, through countless intermediate forms, to limbs that can support life on land.

The labour of generations becomes flesh.

And life does not enter an untouched land. Microbes, fungi, and plants are already changing it. Roots work on stone and help make soil. Plants turn sunlight into living tissue. They change the air and the ground. The later animal steps into a world earlier life helped produce.

Some plants grow taller. In places where reaching above neighbouring plants brings more light, inherited differences in height can matter. Across generations, forests rise and make a vertical world that did not exist before.

Some animals begin living through that world. They climb. They learn where to place their weight. Their young can watch. A solution can pass through behaviour before it ever changes the body.

The whole time, the plant has another problem. How does its future travel if it cannot walk?

The future is in the seed.

In some plant lineages, the seed is wrapped in fruit. The wrapping becomes something another life wants to eat. The animal carries it away. The seed survives the journey and begins again somewhere else.

The animal gets food. The plant gets movement.

So we got fruit from a cell.

Isn’t that beautiful?

A little inside protected by a membrane became a little future protected inside a fruit. Life keeps finding ways to carry what it has produced beyond the place where it was produced.

I was going to call this expansion. Life spreading into the ocean, the land, the air.

No. Not expansion. Connection.

Expansion is only the visible part. Underneath it, one process is being joined to another. The cell joins reactions that used to scatter. The organism joins cells that could not do the same work alone. The fruit joins a tree’s future to an animal’s hunger.

It is the same thing over and over again. A cycle. But the cycle does not return to the same place. Each turn carries forward more accumulated labour, more memory, more of the world brought into relation.

Then the organization that carried all of that can become too small for it.

The chamber kept the chemistry together, but it could not travel. The single cell could travel, but its powers were limited. A body carries many cellular powers together, then finds new limits in the world around it. A species changes the world through its life, and the changed world can make its inherited form a constraint.

The form that protected accumulated labour becomes a box around it.

That is when the movement does not merely make more of the same. It breaks the old arrangement and carries its powers into another one.

We can call that a revolution later. For now, just watch it happen.

The fruit is still hanging in the tree, and the animal is climbing toward it.

The hand that grips a branch can grip something else.

A stone.

The stone can break a shell. It can cut. It can dig. Then the hand changes the stone itself. An edge is shaped and kept.

Now the labour does not disappear when the hand stops moving. The tool remains.

The next hand begins from the work of the last hand. Time shortens. A discovery that may have taken years to reach can be taken up in a moment by someone who inherits the tool.

Past labour stands beside living labour.

The same happens with shelter. Work done yesterday still blocks the rain tonight. A path remembers where feet have gone. Fire continues a process outside the body.

Then voice carries the result farther. One person can warn another about something that is not here. A technique can cross from one mind to another.

Art is a means to remember.

A hand on a cave wall says someone was here. A painted animal holds a hunt after the animal has run away. The image lets another person begin from an experience they did not personally live.

Labour has begun storing itself everywhere.

In tools. In paths. In stories. In the changed land. Humanity does not have to wait for every power to become part of our DNA. We build powers into the world around us, and that worked world changes what the next human can become.

Life has produced something able to look back at life.

Along this one branch, humanity becomes what I call a vanguard of life. I mean only that we can look ahead consciously, not that we have any greater right to exist. Nothing else was waiting to become us. A whale is not an unfinished human.

The power to foresee harm gives us an obligation to life, not a title above it. Life has organized a form that can study an atmosphere, foresee an impact, repair a forest, and consciously carry living processes somewhere they have never reached.

But we are not only the resolution. We are contradicted within ourselves.

The same power can clear the forest, poison the ocean, and turn every other living thing into material for us. Humanity oppresses the life that produced it.

So humanity is not the end of the story. Humanity is part of life.

And this is a story of life.

01

When labour stands against the labourer

Well. Let’s continue.

Here is where I could cheat without noticing.

Cells organize. Gravity gathers. Therefore communism?

No. That does not follow.

Nature contains predators and parasites. It contains cooperation too, and indifferent extinction. A defender of capitalism could select different images from the same world and pretend nature had proven private property.

Nothing I have said about cells and stars proves communism. It only gives me a way to think about dependence and inheritance, about boundaries, and about organizations that can preserve a common power or turn against it.

The political argument has to begin somewhere else. Human production has become social, while control over its conditions and products remains private and alienated. Millions make a common power that returns to them as the property of a minority.

Human beings do not only inherit a body. We inherit a worked world. The child arrives after the path has been cleared, the field has been planted, the word has been spoken, the tool has been shaped.

That inheritance lets human powers grow unbelievably fast. We can gather more food. Sustain more people. Build larger settlements. Divide complicated work among many hands.

But division does two things at once.

It joins our activity into a greater productive power, and it separates each person from much of the power they help produce.

One person plants. Another builds. Someone keeps the record. Someone carries a weapon. Each activity depends on the rest. Society becomes an organism of labour, but unlike the body, the people doing the work do not necessarily share control of the common life they make.

The factory is only the most visible clock. Before anyone reaches it, somebody has fed a child, cleaned a room, cared for a sick body. Much of that labour is done by women without a wage.

Somebody grows the food. Somebody moves it through warehouses and ports. A migrant worker may care for one household while living apart from her own. Capital depends on all this hidden work, and on the colonial and racialized force that has made whole regions give up labour and materials cheaply.

The stored grain can be taken. The land can be enclosed. The weapon made to defend a community can be turned inward to command it.

The product of labour begins standing over the labourer.

Now there is another kind of shortening and lengthening.

The person who commands another person’s labour can shorten the time that belongs to them. Hurry up. Work faster. Produce more before the light is gone. Then the working day itself can be lengthened.

Capitalism did not invent command over time. It takes both movements, locks them together, and makes accumulation from them.

It makes the task faster while keeping the worker at the task for as long as it can. A machine shortens the time needed to make the product, then that saved time appears not as freedom but as a demand for more products. Faster machinery does not automatically give the worker a shorter day. It lets capital pack more labour into every hour.

Work longer. Work harder. The clock is doing both.

This is the bathroom and living room again, except the demand is no longer simply mine, and the time being commanded belongs to millions of people.

Capital is accumulated labour, but accumulated on the other side of the worker. The factory, the machine, the software, the whole inheritance of social knowledge confronts living labour as somebody else’s property.

Humanity made these powers. Then the powers return as commands.

You can feel why capitalism is so difficult to defeat by argument alone. It is not only an idea. It arrives through the senses as the greatest explosion of productive power in history. Trains, medicines, games, machines, towers, global communication. Capital gathers centuries of labour and places its results into everyday life.

People experience the power and are told the power belongs to capital.

That is the trick. Capitalism develops labour with astonishing force while separating the people who labour from the powers they create.

And the larger this organization becomes, the more the people inside it can recognize one another.

Two scattered workers can be ignored. Thousands working through the same system can stop the system.

So the power commanding labour has to organize too.

Weapons become a state. Record-keeping becomes administration. The ruling ideas are repeated until the arrangement feels like nature. When workers speak openly, they can be watched. When they are watched, they speak in codes. When one organization is broken, another begins underground.

It is extremely reminiscent of DNA.

A message survives because it can travel through a hostile world. A lesson from one defeat is carried into another struggle. The people who are gone do not disappear completely if the organization remembers.

But me talking is not enough to solve a contradiction.

You need to organize.

And organization is not automatically liberation. I keep returning to the body because the body makes that obvious.

A nervous system coordinates distant parts. An immune system recognizes danger. The brain receives signals and sends activity back through the whole. This organized power allows the body to exist.

But cancer shows the opposite movement. Cancer is not the revolt of a worker against the body. It is a part that stops contributing to the common life while drawing more and more from it. It recruits blood vessels, consumes nourishment, and makes its own continuation the only purpose until it endangers the organism that keeps it alive.

Capital is much closer to that.

A bureaucracy can become like that too. An organization created to preserve a movement can begin preserving itself instead. Its offices remain. Its ranks remain. Its rules remain. The living purpose drains away while the structure keeps feeding.

So the answer cannot be organization as a sacred word. The question is what the organization is organizing, whose labour it carries, and whether its parts can still govern the whole they produce.

The working class is revolutionary because it is already joined through the labour that makes modern society run, yet it can only free itself by ending the private command of that common power.

It cannot become free by turning every worker into a private capitalist. The relation would simply begin again.

Its emancipation requires the productive inheritance of humanity to become common, which means its victory can open the liberation of everyone.

That is why the proletariat has to become more than a population counted by statistics. It has to become an organized class, capable of taking hold of production, disarming the old ruling power, and governing the transition itself.

The Communist Party is not a name that makes this true. It is the organization that must preserve the clearest memory of the movement, connect struggles that remain scattered, and help the class act as one power without replacing the class with itself.

If it stops returning power to the working class, the form has turned against its purpose.

The dictatorship of the proletariat is the working class organized as the ruling force. It is not the final human community. It is the force required to break the old command over labour, defend the revolution, and reorganize production so that class rule can actually disappear.

The word dictatorship sounds like the rule of one person because that is how we hear it now. Here it names which class holds power. Under capital, a minority rules through property even when everyone votes. In the transition, the immense majority must rule over the forces trying to restore that property.

This is not violence worshipped for itself. Neither is it a promise that the old power will politely vanish. A ruling class has armies, offices, money, law, and habits built through generations. The proletariat needs organized force because disorganized goodness cannot defeat organized domination.

But the force has a direction. It is strongest when it makes its own permanence unnecessary.

The working day shortens. Necessary labour is shared. Knowledge travels. Planning allows the time saved by machinery to return to the people whose accumulated labour made the machinery possible.

But you cannot shorten the day in the factory by lengthening it at home. If women do the care for free, or migrant workers carry it for everyone else, one person’s freedom is still being made from another person’s stolen time.

The proletariat’s interest is to organize time differently.

Not time as a resource owned by a boss. Time to rest without guilt. Time to learn without preparing to be sold. Time to raise a child and still belong to the wider world. Time for relationships that are not squeezed into whatever remains after work.

The point is not to make everyone equally poor in free time. It is to turn the shortening of necessary labour into a lengthening of human life.

The more freely associated labour can do in less time, the more time can become love.

02

Not just for humans

Suppose we succeed.

The factories are no longer private kingdoms. Production is planned for use. The classes that lived by commanding other people’s labour have been abolished. Humanity can finally direct its accumulated powers consciously.

Is that the end?

No.

Humanity may resolve its contradiction within itself and still remain a contradiction to life.

We have treated the Earth like an outside that exists to be taken into us. Forest becomes timber. Animal becomes meat. River becomes power, transport, waste disposal. The vanguard of life becomes an authority over life.

If the deer could speak to us, it could ask a very simple question: Why do you build a house only for yourselves?

Build a house for the deer.

Not literally one human house with a tiny door. Restore the paths it needs. Give the forest enough room to become a forest rather than a decoration around human production.

Make plants the liveliest they could ever be.

We have already changed soils, waters, and climates. There is no innocent path where humanity simply disappears and nature returns to a past untouched by us. Repair is labour too. We have to use the powers that damaged living systems to help them become capable of flourishing beyond our immediate use.

And this cannot mean telling most of humanity to accept misery for the planet while the old centres of wealth keep the world’s labour gathered around themselves.

Poorer regions still need more homes, energy, medicine, transport, and productive capacity. Richer regions can stop pouring human and natural wealth into waste. The question is not whether production rises or falls as one number. The question is what grows, what disappears, and whose life the plan is built to serve.

Communism should not flatten the world into grey sameness.

It should release a greater variety of life than profit can tolerate. More kinds of food because agriculture no longer has to force every seed toward the best return. More beautiful places because public life is not whatever remains after land has been sold. More developed senses because people have time to cook, listen, build, taste, and learn.

A true human community does not finish at the edge of the human species.

Life produced us. The power concentrated in us has to return to life as care.

Then maybe it really does begin to look like heaven on Earth.

Not just for humans, but for everyone.

But no. Go further.

03

Go back to the Sun

Let’s go back to the Sun, and let’s go back to space.

Even a healed Earth is not outside history.

An asteroid can arrive without hatred and end whole branches of life. The Sun itself is changing. Long before it becomes a red giant, its increasing brightness will make this planet harder to inhabit.

Life has survived these dangers blindly before. Humanity can see some of them coming.

That is why we study the sky.

Planetary defence looks like a strange luxury until you remember what an impact has already done to life. Then telescopes become eyes grown by the Earth to watch for what the animal eye cannot see. A spacecraft sent to change an asteroid’s path becomes life reaching outside the planet to protect the planet.

We love looking at the stars. We love looking at the planets. We make constellations. We make stories.

Maybe wonder is not separate from survival either. The night sky enters imagination long before we have the tools to cross it. Desire reaches ahead and starts preparing a human power that does not exist yet.

Photosynthesis already showed the movement in miniature. A cell caught sunlight and built that energy into living matter. Imagine associated humanity doing the same around a star.

A Dyson sphere is photosynthesis becoming architecture.

Not necessarily one solid shell, and not something we can build now. Think of a vast organization of collectors drawing on a larger share of the Sun’s energy. The means of production would no longer stop at the surface of a planet.

Then Earth itself can become a seed.

Life carries samples of the world that produced it into another world. But Mars immediately gives us another contradiction. Do we bring life there only as material subordinated to human settlement? Do we repeat the same domination under another sky?

Terraforming cannot just mean making another planet useful to us. The deeper project would be making a dead world capable of joining life.

How will the tree survive? What will the soil need? Which relations among living things have to travel together so none of them is only cargo for the others?

The lesson from the cell returns. A life does not survive because one part is carried alone. It survives through an organization of conditions.

Go further, go further.

One planet is not enough protection against every danger. One solar system is not the whole future. If life continues, it will separate across planets, then perhaps across stars. Distance will produce difference. A human society leaving Earth could become something that no longer calls itself human after enough generations.

Life will branch again.

Some branches may cooperate. Some may conquer. There may be alliances across solar systems and wars between forms of life whose common origin has become almost impossible to recognize.

The cell became the organism. The organism became a species. The species became a society. Why would the movement stop politely at the border of the atmosphere?

04

Everything and nothing

This is where I am going to speculate.

I am not going to pretend the next part has already been proven. I am also not going to stop thinking merely because the instruments have not caught up with the question.

What is life moving into when it leaves a planet?

Space.

Space separates. Gravity gathers.

I know gravity does not love and space does not hate. They do not need little minds for the movement to matter. I am giving them human words because human words are what I have.

The universe expands. Matter gathers into stars and planets. Everywhere I look, separation and connection are happening together.

So I began calling them nothing and everything.

Nothing is the distance opening between things. Everything is the matter and energy still entering relation. Nothing spreads. Everything organizes.

Not because matter has a secret political opinion. Because a universe with gravity does not remain an even mist. Matter gathers, ignites, collapses, and makes worlds.

The first organization is not life.

Before the cell gathered chemistry behind a membrane, gravity was already gathering matter. A star holds an enormous process together. Around it, a disk becomes planets. On at least one planet, dead matter organizes into life.

Then life becomes the part of matter that can organize deliberately.

Maybe humanity can become life’s vanguard in that limited sense. Maybe life can become the vanguard of everything.

Matter has produced a form capable of asking what threatens matter, what separates it, and how new connections might be built.

The universe is expanding faster and faster. We call whatever drives that dark energy, though we still do not know what it is or whether it will keep behaving this way. If the acceleration continues, galaxies we can see now will eventually pass beyond any signal we could ever receive. They would not be breaking the light barrier. Space itself would be growing between us.

So what does life do?

Under the physics we know, an object with mass cannot simply accelerate through the speed of light. Fine. Then the problem is not to build a faster rocket in the ordinary way. The problem is whether life can learn to work on spacetime itself.

We do not have that power. The contradiction only tells us the kind of power that would be required. It does not give us the machine.

But I refuse to treat permanent separation as the only future we are allowed to imagine.

Life crossed boundaries that once contained everything it could do. Water to land. Body to tool. Planet to space. Each crossing required a form of labour that the earlier life could not perform.

Perhaps faster-than-light travel is impossible in every form. Perhaps a future understanding finds a path through wormholes or some structure we cannot name. The honest answer is that we do not know.

The revolutionary answer is to keep the question alive.

Because if nothingness expands faster than connection, everything we love is gradually placed beyond reach.

And if life finds a way to bridge that separation, the universe gains a new power of connection through us.

05

The universe in rehearsal

This is why science fiction matters to me.

Star Wars is not a scientific forecast. Rick and Morty is definitely not an instruction manual. But fiction lets human beings rehearse contradictions before history forces us to live them.

We already imagine galactic empires because we know what happens when power expands faster than common control. We imagine federations and rebellions because we know no empire remains alone. We imagine a council policing time because the moment time travel appears in a story, somebody tries to regulate the contradictions it creates.

The costumes are from the future. The relations are already ours.

A Death Star is accumulated labour turned into a weapon against the worlds that produced it. A galactic rebellion is scattered life discovering that separate resistance is not enough. A portal gun is the fantasy of defeating distance immediately.

Science fiction is theory written for the future as entertainment.

Not prophecy in the sense that every spaceship will arrive exactly as drawn. Prophecy in the more interesting sense that imagination reveals which future contradictions we can already feel from here.

Suppose life spreads across the galaxy.

Its branches will not stay identical. Different gravity, ecologies, histories, and social systems will make them stranger to each other. One civilization may meet another after each has lived alone for a million years.

What happens then?

Maybe war. Maybe trade. Maybe they are so different that even recognizing each other as life becomes a theoretical achievement.

And maybe the first contact is not between equals. One may have crossed the stars while the other has barely left its ocean. The old question returns in a larger form: does the more organized power make the other world part of a common life, or merely turn it into material?

Galactic communism cannot mean one species painting every planet the same colour.

It would mean creating relations in which radically different life can contribute to a common world without one form becoming the owner of all the others.

I know how ridiculous that sounds while people on Earth still go hungry.

But the distance between hunger and a galaxy is filled by the same problem. Who controls accumulated labour? What is it organized to do? Can the power made by many return to them as a common power?

If we cannot answer that in a factory, we will carry the failure into space.

If we can answer it here, then a future civilization begins with a different inheritance.

06

A seed in the dark

And then there are black holes.

I did not know what to do with them at first.

They are organization taken to an extreme. Matter gathers so densely that a boundary forms beyond which nothing returns to us in the ordinary way. The image can look like everything defeating nothing by crushing itself into one place.

Or it can look like a power that has gathered so much it becomes a prison for what it gathered.

That is why a black hole can remind me of capital and bureaucracy at the same time. Not because an astrophysical object has a class position. Because the shape of the relation is familiar. Accumulation becomes so concentrated that the gathered power no longer circulates through the world that made it possible.

But a black hole is not simply a dead vault.

Quantum theory says even a black hole should radiate. So slowly that every empire looks like a blink, it would lose energy as Hawking radiation.

If that prediction is right, then long after the last stars have gone dark, the last black holes would keep leaking faint radiation until they disappear.

The final fireworks display in a dark universe.

So I keep seeing a fruit.

Matter gathered behind a surface. Information we cannot simply read. Something that survives after the world around it has become cold.

I do not mean a black hole is literally a seed waiting to grow a better universe. We do not yet know exactly how its information is preserved or returned. But as an image, it refuses to leave me.

A fruit carries a future through an animal. DNA carries a past through a body. A book carries a voice through death. A black hole carries the question of matter’s history into the farthest future physics can presently describe.

What looks like an ending may still be a form of storage we do not understand.

And what looks like perfect organization may also be collapse.

The difference is whether accumulation can return to the wider life as a power, or whether it only gathers into itself.

07

To shorten time

Okay. Back to time.

I began with time before I knew I was beginning with time.

I hurried you. You delayed. I tried to shorten the moment between the bathroom and the living room. You lengthened it.

Capital does the same thing on a different scale. It shortens the time needed to produce while lengthening the time during which the worker must produce. It makes every second more intense and still asks for more seconds.

The proletariat fights to shorten the working day.

That sounds small beside galaxies and black holes, but it is not. To win back an hour is to take a piece of human life out of the command of capital. To shorten necessary labour is to lengthen the time in which people can become something other than labour-power.

The future enters the present through every tool. The past enters through accumulated knowledge. But under capital, both arrive as somebody else’s property and command the worker now.

Communism brings the times back into relation.

The past is not a dead weight owned by the capitalist. It is the common inheritance of labour. The future is not a market forecast deciding which life deserves investment. It belongs to the people whose present activity will make it.

Past, present, and future become valid together.

Not identical. Connected.

Then I begin imagining time itself.

We draw it as a line because one moment seems to follow another. But what if reality contains other lines? Other histories produced by choices, accidents, or structures we do not understand?

Current science does not tell us that we can travel among branching timelines. That is where the thought returns to fiction.

Rick and Morty gives us councils trying to control the mess. Back to the Future gives us a life changed because one relationship in the past moved differently. Every time-travel story discovers that entering the past is also an act of production. The traveller works on history, and the changed history works back on the traveller.

Of course it does. Even fantasy cannot escape labour.

If timelines could meet, they would not meet innocently. There would be alliances and domination. Some histories would try to treat others as mistakes. A council might declare one timeline proper and every deviation dangerous.

Then the rebels arrive.

Maybe this never becomes technology. It is still doing theoretical work now. It asks whether one history has the right to erase another, whether contradiction produces only disorder, and whether different possible worlds can be connected without one becoming the prison of the rest.

The goal is not a single flat timeline in which every difference has been deleted.

It is a form capable of holding difference without turning difference into separation.

How do I picture that?

08

Draw an area

You have to start from the abstract.

Draw an area.

At first I see a line. One direction, then the opposite. To lengthen and to shorten. Attraction and separation. Everything and nothing.

Then another line crosses it.

Now there is a plane. Movement is no longer trapped in one direction. Draw a boundary around part of it and you get an inside, like a membrane again. A square contains an area, but the outside remains beyond its edge.

So I keep adding planes. Different directions. Different histories. Different ways the same contradiction could move.

And in my mind they begin curving around one another.

A sphere.

Not because stacking planes mathematically proves that the universe is a sphere. It does not. This is the image my thought makes when it tries to hold all directions without giving one of them the final edge.

The sphere becomes a unity of zero and one.

Zero, nothing. One, something. The empty centre I imagine and the whole form around it. Every point on the surface belongs to the same shape, but no point has to become another point in order to belong.

What is outside the sphere?

Maybe that is the wrong direction to look.

This whole movement has not been waiting for something outside it to arrive. Matter formed stars. Life worked on matter. Humanity worked on life’s inheritance. Every new power was produced inside the relation, then turned back upon the world that produced it.

So God does not have to be the answer standing before everything.

God can be what associated life builds from everything it inherits.

Think about what technology has meant all the way through this essay. The membrane is a power life made for holding an inside together. DNA carries the work of earlier life into the next life. A tool carries the work of one hand into another. Machinery carries the accumulated knowledge of society into production.

Technology is labour preserving itself, organizing itself, and returning as a greater power.

Follow that movement far enough.

Humanity learns to work on the chemistry of a cell, then the ecology of a planet. A later life may learn to work on the energy of a star. Go further still, and associated life may learn to work consciously on the physics that once appeared as its absolute limit.

Space. Time. Gravity.

The most advanced technology humanity can wield would not merely move objects inside the universe. It would control the physical relations that decide how matter moves and how life can exist.

That is God.

Not magic appearing from nowhere. Not an owner of the universe sitting outside the labour that made it possible. God as accumulated labour raised into a conscious common power over the conditions of existence itself.

The cell could not control the ocean. It could only survive one little part of it. Humanity cannot control the universe now. We barely control our own production without turning it against ourselves. But the distance between the cell and us is already the history of life taking more of its conditions into its own activity.

God is not the guaranteed end of that history. It is the furthest possibility I can see inside it.

Capital is accumulated labour returning as an alien command. God is accumulated labour returning to life as its loving common power.

And God cannot be private property.

If one class owns the machinery that controls matter, that is capital expanded to the universe. If one bureaucracy commands time and space above every living being, that is not heaven. It is the oldest separation wearing the most advanced technology.

The power becomes God only when it is the common power of the lives that produced it.

Not one species ruling every other species. Not one planet deciding the form of every world. Everything becoming capable of consciously holding everything in relation.

And it is loving.

It has to be.

Because love is the form of connection that does not need to erase the other side. Conquest connects by swallowing. Property connects by exclusion. Capital connects the whole planet, but only by making the connection obey accumulation.

Love makes another life’s freedom part of its own.

A technology powerful enough to reshape physics but unable to love would only perfect domination. It could cross every distance while leaving everyone separated. It could bring every point in the universe under one command and still fail to make everything one.

The loving God we build does something different. It uses power to make connection possible without turning difference into ownership.

Then the sphere matters because it gives me a way to picture that totality. Everything and nothing are not finally destroyed. They are held in one relation.

This whole sphere is a cycle. It is all contradictions harmonized.

Harmonized does not mean frozen. A dead world has no contradiction because it has nothing living enough to move. That is not the peace I want.

I mean a living world where contradiction no longer has to harden into domination. Difference remains. Push and pull remain. But neither side has to destroy the other in order for the whole to continue.

This is where God, the sphere, and heaven finally meet.

God is the common power capable of making the relation. The sphere is the whole relation held together. Heaven is what living inside it feels like.

Not another world offered as compensation for accepting misery in this one. Not a reason to leave oppression untouched because everything will be repaired after death.

Heaven as a material relation we build.

The working day has become short enough for time to feel whole again. The past is remembered without ruling the living. The future belongs to everyone who must inhabit it.

And if time itself one day becomes something associated life can work on, then even this division may change. The past, present, and future would no longer stand as sealed rooms. The lives cut apart by time might enter relation in ways that are impossible for us now.

I do not know what that technology would be. But now the desire has a place inside the thought. To conquer time would not mean deleting the past or making one eternal present. It would mean connecting what separation made unreachable.

Humanity is reconciled with the life that produced it. Life carries the Earth outward without making the universe another colony. The world-making power returns to everything as care.

We build God from the accumulated labour of life.

Then this God builds the conditions in which life can become more than we were able to imagine.

We make God, and God makes us back.

Not rest forever.

Freedom to keep becoming.

09

Put it back down to you

I have gone from a bathroom to the end of the stars.

From a cell to a worker. From the working day to the age of black holes. From one line to a sphere.

Maybe I have gone too far. Fine. Put it back down to you.

Ask yourself: what do you want to do after realizing it?

I can and I cannot.

That’s it. Nothing and everything.

I can pull you into the room. I cannot make the pulling become closeness. A class can seize production. It cannot call the seizure communism if people remain separated from their own common power. Humanity can reach another planet. It cannot call the planet liberated if every other life arrives as its servant.

Power is real. So is the relation the power produces.

The final answer cannot be connection as conquest.

It has to be love.

I do not mean love as a soft feeling pasted over contradiction. I mean the practical activity of making another life’s freedom part of the condition of your own.

Love listens because the other person is not raw material for your intention. Love organizes because a feeling that cannot alter the conditions hurting someone remains helpless. Love fights when domination has organized itself and will not move for a request.

This is why force and love are not always opposites.

The dictatorship of the proletariat can become the most organized form of love humanity has yet produced, but only if its force is actually directed toward ending the relations that make such force necessary. If it preserves itself above the class, it becomes another separation.

The proletariat carries a possibility larger than itself because its liberation requires humanity’s accumulated labour to belong to humanity.

Humanity carries a possibility larger than itself because the conscious liberation of labour can return our common power to life.

Life carries a possibility larger than Earth because it can take dead matter and make another living world.

And everything carries the possibility of connection because here, in us, matter has begun to organize consciously against separation.

The final communist argument will not be a paragraph.

It will be somebody walking into a new social relation and saying: Holy shit. Look what we can do when our labour belongs to us.

That is how everything fights nothing.

Not by becoming one enormous thing that crushes every difference inside itself. By building a relation in which difference can remain connected, memory can become a common inheritance, and power can return to the lives that made it.

To love is to organize against separation.

To organize is to give love a material power.

I have to love. I have to forgive. I have to cry.

Crying is connection.

And after all this, after the cell and the worker and the star and the sphere, the answer becomes embarrassingly simple.

I just want to love.
